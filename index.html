Layer 11 — Attention Engineering
"Social media doesn't addict you by giving you something good. It addicts you by making you feel like something great might happen."
Dopamine spikes harder in anticipation than in reward. The Sapolsky experiment showed dopamine levels doubled when a reward was only possible 50% of the time — compared to when it was certain. This is the slot machine principle. It's also why you can't stop scrolling.

VYRALL uses this the other way. Three mechanics applied to every script:
Near-Miss Structure
At the midpoint, the payoff feels imminent but isn't delivered yet. "I'll tell you exactly what this means — but first you need to see why..." This compels completion.
Variable Reward Pacing
Insights drop unevenly — early spike, then withhold, then another drop, then payoff faster than expected. Unpredictable spacing doubles the dopamine response vs evenly-spaced content.
Zero-Friction Entry
The hook requires zero prior context. A mid-scroll viewer has 1.5 seconds. Any hook that needs setup has already lost. VYRALL tests every hook against this before outputting.
Hook categories VYRALL selects from: "You're Being Manipulated" · Anticipation Gap · Near-Miss Reveal · System Expose · Identity Reframe · Consequence Preview. The AI selects whichever hits hardest for the specific topic — shown in the analysis card after every generation.

Audience Persona Builder
Five questions. Set once. Every output becomes sharper — forever.
Question 1
Who is your ideal viewer?
Age, occupation, life situation. Be specific — not "entrepreneurs" but "female founders aged 28-40 running service businesses in the UK."
Question 2
What is their primary pain point?
The thing that keeps them up at night. The frustration they feel every week. Not the surface problem — the real one underneath it.
Question 3
What have they already tried that hasn't worked?
Failed solutions matter. Mentioning what didn't work signals you understand their journey — not just their problem.
Question 4
What do they secretly want to believe about themselves?
This is the identity beneath the problem. The self-image they're reaching toward. Content that reflects this back to them is the content they share.
Question 5
What sentence would make them stop scrolling and say "that's exactly me"?
One sentence. Specific enough to feel written for them personally. This becomes the invisible north star for every hook VYRALL writes.

Virality Intelligence Report
The five scores (0–100)
Hook Strength, Emotion Arousal, Share Trigger, Platform Fit, and VRIN Score are pre-flight checks. Any score below 70 is a flag — hover each score label for a specific explanation of what it means and what to do about it.
→ If VRIN Score is below 60, use the Remix Engine to find a rarer angle on the same topic.
VRIN Breakdown (V R I N)
Four sub-scores that make up the VRIN total. Valuable — does it deliver real value. Rare — how uncommon the angle is. Inimitable — how dependent on your specific voice. Non-substitutable — is this the definitive take.
→ Low Rare score means the angle is saturated. Low Inimitable score means anyone could post this — inject more personal POV.
AI Selections — Emotion, Hook Category, Triggers
These are the strategic decisions VYRALL made autonomously — you never choose these yourself. They are shown transparently so you understand why your content is structured the way it is. Hover each label for a full explanation.
→ If the emotion or hook category feels wrong for your voice, use the Emotion Shift tool or regenerate with a different tone setting.
Content Structure
The step-by-step architecture of your script — Hook → Perspective Shift → Near-Miss Bridge → Core → Self-Reflection → Payoff. Each step has a specific psychological job. The Near-Miss Bridge is the moment that compels completion.
Your Script
This is the final version — not a draft
VYRALL does not produce starting points. The script is publish-ready. Read it aloud before filming — adjust any words that don't feel natural in your voice. The structure stays. The exact phrasing is yours to own.
For video platforms (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts)
This is your teleprompter script. Deliver it to camera. Do not read it verbatim — learn it well enough to make it feel natural. The line breaks are pacing marks, not paragraph breaks.
→ Use the Shot List tool to know exactly how to film each section.
For LinkedIn and text platforms
Paste the script directly as your post. The line breaks are intentional — they create the white space LinkedIn's algorithm and readers reward. Do not consolidate into solid paragraphs.
→ Choose one of the five Caption Variants as your opening two lines — the text visible before "see more" cuts off.
